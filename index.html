Campaign Shows Why

The Chanel Iconic campaign featuring Rianne van Rompaey.

© Inez & Vinoodh

Chanel’s new handbag campaign, entitled “The Chanel Iconic”, shines the
spotlight on one of the French house’s forever classics: the 11.12. Commonly
referred to as the Chanel Flap bag, the quilted shoulder style takes the
blueprint of the 2.55 bag and gives it a twist – literally. While the 2.55
features a rectangular lock, the 11.12 fastens shut with a double-C turn-style
clasp. The 2.55’s chain-link strap is replaced with a metal chain interwoven
with leather on a functional-yet-decorative day-to-day accessory, or an
“accomplice”, as it’s affectionately described by the brand.

Chanel acolytes will naturally be able to recite the history of the 2.55 – from
the specific purpose of the seven discreet pockets to the quilting inspired by
the padded jackets beloved in the equestrian world. Born in February 1955
(hence its 2.55 moniker), it was not until the ’80s that the 11.12 iteration rose
in popularity under the creative helm of Karl Lagerfeld. “Eternally elegant,
the 11.12 felt cunningly familiar due to being influenced by the iconic 2.55
bag,” says the brand of its ability to revamp its signatures, while retaining the
same sense of timelessness at Chanel’s core.


“If you’re sad, add more lipstick and attack,” Coco Chanel once said.
Thus, one of the seven inner pockets of the 2.55 and the 11.12 is
still dedicated to mood-boosting cosmetics.

© Chanel

The pandemic has spurred on fashion’s advancement towards a greener
future, in which the season a garment belongs to no longer defines it, and
thus, the Chanel Iconic campaign couldn’t feel more timely. As the label
neatly puts it in its description of the 11.12, the Flap bag is “a rare case of
practicality and ingenuity balanced by allure”: the perfect combination and
the ultimate investment purchase.
